✅ verifAI

Is your paper safe to submit?

arXiv and major venues now reject papers with AI-written leftovers and made-up references. verifAI finds ChatGPT artifacts and hallucinated citations in your .tex / .bib / .pdf — and hands you fixed files.

Even if you only used AI to polish grammar, it can hallucinate silently — swap a year, misspell an author, invent a reference that never existed. verifAI is a deterministic recheck (zero AI inside): it flags obvious AI-generated text and verifies every citation against four scholarly databases — catching even partially hallucinated ones (real paper, wrong year, wrong authors, dead DOI).

🔒 100% in your browser 🚫 zero generative AI 🗄 4 scholarly databases 🆓 free & open source
What it catches — real examples:
not found \cite{smith2023quantum} “Recursive Quantum Embedding Fusion…” — unknown to Semantic Scholar, CrossRef, OpenAlex & DBLP, DOI doesn’t resolve → hallucinated, removed
high “Certainly! Here is the revised introduction.” chat artifact left in the paper → sentence removed
fixable year = {2021 → 2019} wrong year → corrected from the matched record

⚖️ verifAI gives you leads, not verdicts — every fix is a suggestion you review and can override. Your files never leave the browser; only citation titles are sent to public scholarly APIs.

    1 · AI-generated-text findings

    SeverityFileLineProblemEvidence (sentence)Fix

    High-severity chat artifacts are pre-selected for removal. Low-severity stylistic flags are never auto-removed — real authors use those phrases too. Matches inside quotation marks, tables or verbatim blocks are treated as deliberate examples.

    2 · Citation verification

    StatusKeyCited asChecked againstProblem / proposed correction Action on generate ⓘ

    Action on generate is what happens to each entry when you press “Generate fixed files”: keep unchanged (nothing happens) · auto-correct fields (year/DOI/authors fixed from the matched record) · delete entry + its \cite (removed from the .bib and every \cite command). It is preset from the status and you can override it. “Not found” across all four databases (Semantic Scholar, CrossRef — which carries the full ACM catalog —, OpenAlex, DBLP — which carries the ACL Anthology) is the classic signature of a fabricated citation. Books, websites, software and theses are marked unverifiable, never auto-removed. For unmatched entries, use the search ↗ links in the row to check Google Scholar, ACM DL or ResearchGate manually (those sites block automated queries, so verifAI can’t query them for you).
